About TSA

  • The Technology Student Association (TSA) is a national, non-profit organization of students engaged in science, technology, engineering and mathematics (STEM). Currently, TSA's membership includes over 250,000 middle and high school students in approximately 2,000 schools spanning 48 states delegation. Organized by teachers, parents, and business leaders, this event allows for TSA members to explore interdisciplinary education through a range of competitive events and leadership opportunities. The diversity of activities makes TSA a positive experience for every student exploring STEM careers.

  • TSA members have the opportunity to compete in events through the annual State conferences as well as the annual National conference. The members are able to gain recognition as well as new connections by displaying their talents and efforts through their project.

  • The students from the Cascade High School chapter has competed in many events over the years, some of which are Technology Bowl, Video Game Design, On Demand Video, Essays on Technology, Dragster Design, Coding, Debating Tech Issues, as well as Engineering Design.




Mission Statement

The Technology Student Association fosters personal growth, leadership, and opportunities in Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM); members apply and integrate these concepts through co-curricular activities, competitions, and related programs.

Creed

I believe that Technology Education holds an important place in my life in the technical world. I believe there is a need for the development of good attitudes concerning work, tools, materials, experimentation, and processes of industry. ONGOING SUCCESS


Growth


The TSA chapter at Cascade High School has been gradually gaining new members since its establishment. Within the 2017-2018 school year, the club has grown to 21 members.





Community Service


The TSA chapter at Cascade has contributed to helping out the community in several ways. One of the major events we helped set up and provided volunteers for is the Innovation Expo in Everett for the past two years. The Innovation Expo is a showcase of STEM activities in various schools within the Everett area as well as a competition among students in the fourth and fifth grades as well as middle school students. They showcase research, experimental, and design projects of which they worked throughout the year on.
